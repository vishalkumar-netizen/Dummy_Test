What is TAT in SSB? A Complete Guide

The Thematic Apperception Test (TAT) is the very first test conducted on Day 2 of the SSB Interview. It is the backbone of the psychological assessment series and plays a massive role in determining your recommendation.

How is TAT conducted?

In the testing hall, you will be shown a series of 12 pictures on a projector. The pictures will be flashed one after another.

  • Each picture is shown for exactly 30 seconds.
  • You get exactly 4 minutes to write a story about it.
  • The 12th slide will be completely blank, where you must write a story based entirely on your own imagination.

The Golden Formula for a TAT Story

The psychologist is not looking for a Hollywood movie script. They are looking to see your subconscious mind at work. Every good TAT story must have these three elements:

  1. What led to the situation? (The Past): Briefly explain how the characters arrived at the scene shown in the picture.
  2. What is happening right now? (The Present): This is the core action. What is your main character (the hero) doing to solve the problem or handle the situation?
  3. What will be the outcome? (The Future): Always end on a positive, logical note. Your hero must succeed through their own hard work.

Remember: Keep your handwriting clean, don't use highly negative words (like murder or suicide) unless the picture absolutely demands it, and never leave a story incomplete!
